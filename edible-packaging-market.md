<p><p>The edible packaging market is categorized into several types based on material composition. Polysaccharides, derived from natural sources like starch and cellulose, offer biodegradability and versatility. Lipid-based films, made from fats and oils, provide moisture barriers and enhance shelf life. Surfactant films, often utilizing surface-active agents, contribute to improved stability and flexibility. Protein films, sourced from materials like whey or soy, provide strength and barrier properties. Other materials may include composites or innovative blends, expanding the market's diversity and applications.</p></p>
