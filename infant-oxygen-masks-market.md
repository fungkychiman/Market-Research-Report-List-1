<p><p>The infant oxygen masks market is divided into two main types: those with an operation window and those without. Masks with an operation window allow for adjustments and ventilation changes during use, facilitating easier monitoring of the infant's oxygen needs. In contrast, masks without an operation window provide a sealed environment, ensuring a consistent oxygen supply but limiting real-time adjustments. Both types aim to enhance respiratory support for infants, each catering to specific clinical scenarios and requirements for effective oxygen delivery.</p></p>
