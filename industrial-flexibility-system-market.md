<p><p>Industrial flexibility systems are categorized into Sequential and Random Flexible Manufacturing Systems (FMS) based on their production flow and operation efficiency. Sequential FMS follows a predetermined sequence, optimizing tasks in a structured workflow, which enhances predictability and control. In contrast, Random FMS allows for a more dynamic approach, enabling the system to adapt to varying production demands and schedules, enhancing responsiveness. Both types aim to improve efficiency and meet diverse customer needs while minimizing downtime and maximizing resource utilization.</p></p>
