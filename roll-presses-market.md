<p><p>The roll presses market is primarily divided into hot rolling and cold rolling segments. Hot rolling involves shaping metal at elevated temperatures, enhancing ductility and allowing for complex shapes. It is commonly used for large structural components. Conversely, cold rolling takes place at room temperature, yielding tighter tolerances and improved surface finishes, often utilized for thinner materials and components requiring strength and precision. Both methods are essential in various industries, including construction, automotive, and manufacturing, catering to specific material properties and applications.</p></p>
